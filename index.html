Our Outdoor Attractions

  • Matterhorn

    • This enchanting mountain is one of the tallest in the Alps, straddling the border between Switzerland and Italy. It is a large, near-symmetric pyramidal peak in the extended Monte Rosa area of the Pennine Alps, whose summit is 4,478 metres (14,692 ft) high, making it one of the highest summits in the Alps and Europe. The mountain overlooks the beautiful Swiss town of Zermatt, in the canton of Valais, to the north-east and the Italian town of Breuil-Cervinia in the Aosta Valley to the south.

    What you should bring:

    1. Outdoor surivival supplies
    2. Food
    3. Emergency supplies (first aid kit, flares, emergency signaler)
    4. Warm cozy hiking jackets
    5. Oxygen equipment (most likely needed)

    BEWARE: This hike is difficult. Switzerland Tourism is not responsible for any damages caused by this attraction, to people or to items. If you start to become uncomfortable, decend the mountain.

    You most definitely cannot miss this one!

    Cost: 0 CHF (0 CAD)




  • Rhine Falls

    • This gorgeous waterfall is located in Switzerland and the most powerful waterfall in Europe. The falls are located on the High Rhine on the border between the cantons of Schaffhausen and Zürich, between the municipalities of Neuhausen am Rheinfall and Laufen-Uhwiesen/Dachsen , next to the town of Schaffhausen in northern Switzerland. They are 150 metres (490 ft) wide and 23 metres (75 ft) high. The best time to visit is in June and July, when the mountain snow melts and the falls puff and swell, a gorgeous view to see.

    What to bring:

    1. Swim gear
    2. Emergency rescue equipment (like a heat pack in case of hypothermia)
    3. Jacket (it is chilly before you enter)
    4. VERY WARM towels
    5. Extra clothing
    6. Snacks (please do not litter)

    BEWARE: The water can be very cold, and currents may be strong. Switzerland Tourism is not responsible for any damages caused by this attraction, to people or to items.

    You better not miss this marvelous fall!

    Cost: 5 CHF (About 7 CAD)

  • Lake Lugano and Ticino

    • Lake Lugano is stunning glacial lake which is situated on the border between southern Switzerland and northern Italy. The lake, named after the nearby city of Lugano, is situated between the dazzling Lake Como and Lago Maggiore. There are various mountains and tourist destinations on the shores of the lake including Monte Brè to the east, Monte San Salvatore west of Lugano, and Monte Generoso on the south-eastern shore. The World Heritage Site Monte San Giorgio is situated south of the lake. Also located to the south is the Cinque Vette Park. The lake is drained by the Tresa, which empties into Lake Maggiore, the latter being drained by the Ticino and the Po.

    What to bring on this adventure:

    1. Scuba gear
    2. Kayak and paddles
    3. Emergency supplies
    4. Warm blankets
    5. Extra clothing
    6. Life jackets (important)

    BEWARE: The water is deep and cold. We do not recommend this if you are not a good swimmer. Switzerland Tourism is not responsible for any damages caused by this attraction, to people or to items.

    You can't miss this one!

    Cost: 0 CHF (0 CAD)
